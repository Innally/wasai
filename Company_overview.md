# Company Overview
WASAI operates two entities under one roof: the Shuangjiang Wasai Tea Co., Ltd. (founded 2020, the brand/trading arm) and the Shuangjiang Mengku East Half Mountain Ancient Tree Tea Factory (founded 1993, renamed 2010, the production arm). Both are located at Nasai Village, Mengku, Shuangjiang County, Lincang City, Yunnan Province.
The operation is sizable — over 700 contracted farming households, 13,000+ acres of owned and cooperative tea gardens, a 12,000+ m² factory with two primary processing lines and one refined raw pu’er line, and a 5,000+ m² elevated drying platform.
# What Makes It Distinctive
The core differentiation is its positioning as a pure raw material supplier rather than a finished tea brand — serving many of China’s well-known tea brands and companies with custom-produced sun-dried mao cha (晒青毛料). Annual output is a stable 200+ tonnes, with a monthly peak capacity of 40 tonnes of dry leaf.
Several specific practices set it apart:
On sourcing and ecology: Tea garden selection follows multi-generational knowledge, factoring in slope, wind direction, sunlight hours, surrounding vegetation, and soil fertility. The gardens span elevations from 1,300m to 2,000m+, predominantly planted with the Mengku Large-Leaf Vine-Trained cultivar (藤条茶) — a varietal that yields less volume but produces notably higher-quality leaf.
On farming practice: Pesticides and chemical fertilizers are strictly banned across all contracted farms. Garden management is entirely manual — hand-weeding, hand-tilling at least twice per year — with traceability built in through a team-leader accountability system. The philosophy is that every leaf delivered to the factory should be genuinely zero-pesticide-residue.
On processing: The factory uses traditional craft — no “doing green” (做青), no yellowing (闷黄), no wilting (萎凋). The 5,000+ m² elevated drying floor (离地晒场) can process 10,000+ jin of fresh leaf in a single batch, completing the drying cycle within 24 hours. For ripe pu’er (熟茶), fermentation uses a traditional small-pile method, elevated wooden board fermentation, with water drawn from a natural spring 50 meters underground, in a dedicated climate-controlled fermentation room.
On quality verification: Products have passed national-level inspection (National Pu’er Tea Product Quality Inspection Center, CNAS-certified), with 100+ pesticide residues undetected, moisture content within standard, and coliform bacteria well below national limits (<30 vs. national standard of 300).
# Strategic Vision
WASAI frames its ambition around four principles it calls “Four Highs” — high standards, high volume, high quality, and high requirements — across the full chain from cultivation to storage. The longer-term goal is to become the most influential raw material supplier on Mengku’s East Half Mountain, while also serving as a community anchor that drives income growth for local farming households.
The brand tagline captures it well: “One mountain’s inheritance, one cup of purity.”
